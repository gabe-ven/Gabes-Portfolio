**Design taste:**
- Dark, minimal, developer-aesthetic portfolio
- Loves the dark glass panel / glassmorphism look from the hero section — this is the reference point for everything
- Wants things to feel "premium" and "unique" but also "simple and clean"
- Dislikes: cluttered layouts, too many colors, confined box-in-box nesting, terminal-window clichés when overused
- Typography: monospace fonts, code-editor vibes, Press Start 2P for the 8-bit code aesthetic
- Color: mostly monochromatic white/grey; red `#E63946` is the portfolio accent

**Communication style:**
- Short, direct, sometimes all-caps when frustrated ("SIMPLE PLEASE", "NO")
- Typos are common — interpret intent ("cintinuw" = "continue")
- When he says "like that" or "similar to X", he means match the exact visual style/aesthetic of X
- He iterates quickly — expects fast turnaround, gets frustrated with over-explanation

**What to avoid:**
- Don't over-explain changes — he can see the diff
- Don't add features he didn't ask for (skills, extra animations, decorative elements)
- Don't produce intermediate designs when he's being very specific — just build it

**Why:** Learned over a long design iteration session for the about section.
**How to apply:** Read his short messages carefully, build exactly what he described, keep responses tight.
